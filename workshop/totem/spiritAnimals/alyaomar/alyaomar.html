Spirit Animal


The Butterfly

It's pretty unclear whether or not the butterfly was a symbol of who I am or who I am supposed to aspire to be. The description for the butterfly spent a lot of effort in advising ways to look upon the butterfly to be a better person, love myself, and essentially chill the hell out. The advice was pretty terrible. The butterfly is a representation of "metamorphosis and transformation in my personal life" as well as moving through different life cycles. I think in obvious terms, this acceptance of transformation resonates well with the ending of my undergraduate career and the beginning of my professional life, but the changes in my personal life aren't as drastic as this butterfly is making it out to be. I used to deal with anxiety and stress about the uncertainty of my life and where I was going to be in 5, 10 years. Sometime over the course of the last year, largely due to my travels and adventures in South Africa and Namibia, I gained a sense of peace and contentment with my life that I've managed to keep with me every day since. I think my psyche and I are pretty cool as it is. On the other hand, I can appreciate the lighthearted and playfulness that the butterfly also represents (although I've never seen a butterfly that wanted to make friends or chill out for a minute or two, so there's that.)

Alya Omar | IML 300 MA+P | USC
